# PowerBI end of usage

To use PowerBI the way our team had in mind we needed the option to publish graphs online. I started by checking online to see if there was any way to do this without needing a specific license. Pretty quickly, I found out that extra access had to be requested through the HvA portal. I submitted the request but didn’t hear back.

About a week later, I checked the site again and found a phone number for the HvA ICT servicedesk. I called them and asked if there was a way for me, as a student, to upgrade my license or somehow still use the premium version of PowerBI. They pointed me to some embedded desktop version through HvA, but that turned out to be a dead end.

At the end of the call, I asked if there were any other options. They told me to ask my IoT lecturer — in my case, Mats Otten. So, around 10 minutes later, I asked Mats. He immediately told me that he couldn’t help with getting a license either. He also explained that HvA is cutting costs, and PowerBI was one of the things affected. That made sense, because I remember being able to publish dashboards just fine a year or two ago.

For now, together with Darian I will be looking into other options. The first one to go over is Metabase.
